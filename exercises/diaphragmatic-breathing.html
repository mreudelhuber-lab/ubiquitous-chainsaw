Diaphragmatic Breathing High

Coordinate breathing with core activation; reduce sympathetic arousal.

How to

  • One hand on chest, one below ribs. Inhale through the nose so the abdomen rises.
  • Exhale through pursed lips; abdomen falls. Keep neck/shoulders quiet.
  • Position: supine with knees bent, or crook-lying; progress to seated/standing.

See illustrated guides: Cleveland Clinic PDF • Cleveland Clinic Abu Dhabi

Dosage (reps/sets)

  • 5–10 minutes, 1–2×/day.
  • Shorter bouts are fine; quality over volume.

Completion gate

  • 5 minutes comfortable diaphragmatic breathing without neck/shoulder tension.
  • Pain ≤5/10 and returns to baseline ≤2 hours post-session.

Maintenance

  • Keep 2–3 minutes in warm-ups or on recovery days.
  • Use before Phase 2 endurance tests to normalize breathing.

Safety / regressions

  • Dizziness → sit or lie down; shorten sets.
  • If chest rises first, slow down; place hands to cue belly rise.
