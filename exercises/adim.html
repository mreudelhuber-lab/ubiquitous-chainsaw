Abdominal Drawing-In Maneuver (ADIM) Medium

Preferentially recruits the transversus abdominis (TA) while breathing normally.

How to

  • Supine 90/90 (hips/knees); fingers just inside ASIS to feel TA tension.
  • Gently draw navel “up and in” as if zipping tight jeans; keep breathing.
  • No pelvic tilt or bearing down; neck/jaw relaxed.

Evidence & how-to: Teyhen et al., 2005 (JOSPT) • Oh et al., 2007 (JOSPT) • BMC Sports Sci Rehabil 2024 • Allison et al., 2008

Dosage (reps/sets)

  • 10 × 5–10-second holds, 1–2 sets.
  • Progress by increasing control in different positions (supine → quadruped → standing).

Completion gate

  • 10×10-second holds with normal breathing, palpable TA tension, no pelvic tilt.

Maintenance

  • Use as a light primer before Phase 1–2 sessions.
  • Weave into daily tasks (standing, walking) 2–3 brief cues/day.

Safety / regressions

  • If you hold your breath or tilt pelvis, reduce hold time and reset the cue.
  • Try “sniff in, long exhale” to avoid bracing/valsalva.
